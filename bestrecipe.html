Made by combining thinly shredded cabbage and salt, then allowing the mixture to ferment through a natural process of lactic acid bacteria.


Sauerkraut originated in China over 2,000 years ago when workers on the Great Wall fermented cabbage in rice wine to preserve it. The recipe traveled to Europe with the Mongols and Tartars in the 13th century, where it became popular and was adapted by Germans to use local cabbage and salt, eventually taking the name "sauerkraut" (sour cabbage).

  • INGREDIENTS
  • Cabbage
  • Salt
  • INSTRUCTIONS
  • Shred cabbage
  • Put in jar and add salt proportional to cabbage weight
  • Pound the salted cabbage till brine covers the cabbage
  • Pack it in the jar and seal in a dark cool environment
  • Wait time until cabbage is the desirable taste and flavor
